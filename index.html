How We Build a Training Plan

Every plan starts with an assessment, not a template.

A training plan at Pulse never starts with a generic sheet of sets and reps. Before your first session, a coach walks through a movement assessment to understand your current strength, mobility limits, and any past injuries. That assessment shapes the actual program — how heavy you lift, how often you train, and where a physiotherapist needs to be involved before a coach pushes intensity.

Programs are reviewed every four weeks using lift logs and mobility scores, so your plan changes as your body changes, rather than staying fixed for months at a time. Members recovering from an injury follow a coordinated rehab-to-training track, moving from physiotherapy sessions into standard strength or cardio programming only once cleared. It's a slower start for some, but it's the difference between training for a season and training for years.
